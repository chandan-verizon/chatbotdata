A3: High bills may result from exceeding data limits, making international calls, or subscribing to premium services. Review your usage, check for any unauthorized activities, and contact your telecom provider's customer service to discuss the charges and explore potential solutions. Q4: My mobile data isn't working. How can I troubleshoot this? A4: Ensure that mobile data is enabled in your phone settings. Check for any network outages in your area. If the issue persists, try restarting your device or contacting your telecom provider for assistance. They can provide specific troubleshooting steps based on your device and network. Q5: How can I change my Wi-Fi password? A5: Access your router's settings through a web browser by entering its IP address. Log in with the administrator credentials (usually found on the router). Locate the Wi-Fi settings and change the password. If you're unsure, consult your router's manual or contact your service provider for guidance. Q6: What should I do if I have no signal on my mobile phone? A6: Check if there's an outage in your area by contacting your telecom provider or checking their website. Restart your phone and ensure that airplane mode is not enabled. If the issue persists, contact customer support for further assistance. Q7: How can I cancel a service or subscription?
